Johan Cruyff Tribute

Johan Cruyff

1947 - 2016

"In a way I’m probably immortal."

Here is a time line of Johan Cruyff's life:

  • 1947 – Born in Amsterdam, Netherlands
  • 1957 - Officially joined Ajax at his 10th birthday
  • 1964 - Made his first team debut for Ajax in the Eredivisie
  • 1966 - Won the Dutch Eredivisie league for the first time with Ajax
  • 1968 - Married with his wife Danny Coster
  • 1971 - Won the Ballon d'Or for best European player
  • 1971 - Won the European Cup Final with Ajax, their first, by defeating Panathinaikos 2–0
  • 1972 - Ajax won a second European Cup, beating Inter Milan 2–0 in the final, with Cruyff scoring both goals.
  • 1973 - Won the European Super Cup by beating Rangers over two matches.
  • 1973 - Won the third successive European Cup with Ajax by defeating Juventus 1-0
  • 1973 - Won the Ballon d'Or for best European player
  • 1973 - Sold to FC Barcelona for a world record transfer fee
  • 1974 - Won the Ballon d'Or for best European player
  • 1974 - Won the La Liga, the Spanish championship, with Barcelona, their first in 14 years
  • 1974 - Golden Ball for best player of the World Cup tournament in West Germany
  • 1987 - Won the European Cup Winners' Cup Final as manager of Ajax
  • 1989 - Won the European Cup Winners' Cup Final as manager of Barcelona
  • 1991 - Underwent a double heart bypass surgery due to heavy smoking
  • 1991 - Won the La Liga with Barcelona for the first time as manager and was on to win the Spanish championship the following three consecutive years.
  • 1992 - Won the European Cup final as a manager of Barcelona, their first European Cup
  • 1999 - Voted European Player of the Century
  • 2002 - Promoted to Officer of the Order of Orange-Nassau.
  • 2010 - Presented the FIFA Order of Merit
  • 2016 - Prime Minister of Spain awarded Cruyff with the Gold Medal of the Royal Order of Sporting Merit
  • 2016 - Died in Barcelona, Spain

"“I think he’s one of those legends whose name is always going to be spoken about with the Peles and Maradonas. The rest of us, we’re just mortals, we come and we go.” — Jordi Cruyff"
